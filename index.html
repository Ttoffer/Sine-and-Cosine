Complex plane and eiα

The circle is the Gaussian plane: the horizontal axis is the real part, the vertical axis is the imaginary part. The moving tip is the complex number z = r eiα = r cos α + i r sin α with α = θ + φ (same angle as on the diagram). Euler’s identity links rotation to cosine and sine: eiα = cos α + i sin α, so Re(z) = r cos α and Im(z) = r sin α match the teal and coral projections. With time, α(t) = ∫ω dt + φ (or ωt + φ when ω is constant) gives z(t) = r eiα(t) — constant |z| = r; if ω varies, phase advances faster or slower over time.

What you are seeing

A radius of length r starts along the positive x-axis and turns counter-clockwise. The angle from the x-axis is θ. The moving dot is at angle θ + φ on the circle (phase φ rotates the whole picture).

The tip is at (r cos(θ+φ), r sin(θ+φ)). The upper graph plots sin(θ+φ) and/or cos(θ+φ) versus θ (horizontal axis). One full turn in θ is 2π radians — that horizontal span is one wavelength.

The lower panels share the same simulation time t as the integration dθ/dt = ω(t), so θ(t) = ∫₀ᵗ ω(τ) dτ (when ω is constant this is ωt). The top strip plots θ(t); its slope at any instant is ω(t). The bottom strip plots ω(t) — flat when ω is constant, or curved with variation + pattern (sinusoidal FM, or ramp ω up/down each segment). For ramps, the pattern repeats every T_nom ≈ (revolutions × 2π) / ω_base seconds — that is only how often the ramp restarts, not necessarily when θ completes a cycle. When θ reaches revolutions × 2π, both t and the displayed θ reset to 0. Pause and Step θ advance θ and t together.

Controls

  • Show — sine, cosine, or both.
  • Playback — pause/resume; step advances θ by a fixed small amount.
  • Reset (1, 0) — sets θ and t to 0, φ to 0 so the radius lies on the positive x-axis (x=1, y=0 on the unit circle), clears drawn waves, and pauses.
  • Start again — resumes playback from the current θ and t.
  • Angle axis — tick labels under the θ-graph in radians or degrees.
  • Rotation speed — how fast θ advances (and time t on the lower graph).
  • Revolutions per cycle — θ resets after this many turns.
  • Amplitude (r) — scales circle and wave heights.
  • Phase φ — horizontal shift of the waves; rotates the radius on the circle.
  • ω base — centre angular frequency (rad/s); dθ/dt = ω(t).
  • ω variation — 0 keeps ω constant; larger values let the pattern swing ω up/down around the base.
  • ω vs time pattern — constant; sinusoidal ω(t); or ramp ω up/down each segment (segment length ≈ time for one full θ-cycle at ω base, used only to repeat the ramp).
